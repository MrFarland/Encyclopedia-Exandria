Alabaster Sierra Range

Exandria > Tal’Dorei > Alabaster Sierras

Type: Mountain

Aptly named, the Alabaster Sierras are tall, rocky mountains of chalk-white stone. The range stretches over three hundred miles from the base of the Alabaster Peninsula, along the Alabaster Bay and around the peninsula’s northern tip.

The entirety of the Alabaster Peninsula is wild, untamed wilderness and the Sierras are no different. Behirs, harpies, and wyverns call the mountains home, as do a number of stone giant clans. The city of Whitestone is the only outpost of human civilization on the peninsula.

Infused with magical energy, the white stone from which the mountains get their name is a hot commodity in arcane circles. While the raw stone has some magical properties, it’s arcane power is greatly enhanced once it has been refined into a green glass-like substance called residuum. It is a lucrative export for the city of Whitestone and numerous quarries dot the northern end of the Alabaster Sierras.

Canon Consistency: High - (Tal’Dorei Campaign Guide, p. 46)

Alabaster Trail

Exandria > Tal’Dorei > Alabaster Sierras

Type: Forest

The Alabaster Trail is the only road linking the city of Whitestone in northeastern Tal’Dorei to the rest of the continent. It stretches a little over 350 miles from Whitestone in the north to the town of Turst Fields in the eastern Dividing Plains.

The Trail is a dangerous road. The northern and southern ends are well patroled by the Paleguard and Shields of the Plains, but the majority of the road winds through the dark and dangerous Parchwood Timberland.

Bandits, gnolls and orcs are a constant threat on the southern end, but even they avoid the stretch through the forest. Dire beasts and the undead have been known to harass travelers along the road.

Dawnmist Bay

Exandria > Tal’Dorei > Alabaster Sierras

Type: Coastal & Underwater

Located on the western side of the Alabaster Peninsula, the Dawnmist Bay is a large, sheltered bay that drives deep into the continent of Tal’Dorei. While an extension of the Frigid Depths, the Dawnmist Bay is warmer than its sister, the Alabaster Bay on the eastern side of the peninsula.

Type: Forest

Dominating the northeastern corner of Tal’Dorei is the dark and dense Parchwood Timberland. A massive forest, wild, untamed, and largely unexplored. Few dare to stray from the Alabaster Trail travelling between Whitestone and Turst Fields and many who do are never seen again.

The Parchwood Timberland is an extremely dangerous forest that teems with monstrosities, undead, lycans, and corrupted plant-life. Even the gnolls of eastern Tal’Dorei, like the Moonsteeth and Priest-Eaters stick to the southern edge of the forest.

The Shearing Channel is a narrow passage between the continents of Tal’Dorei in the west and Wildemount in the east. It connects the remote ports on the Frigid Depths from the Lucidian Ocean and the rest of Exandria’s lucrative ports.

Upper Mooren River Run

Exandria > Tal’Dorei > Alabaster Sierras

Type: Forest

The Upper Mooren River Run begins its nearly 600 mile journey high in the Alabaster Sierras and ends in the town of Drynna on the northwestern shore of Mooren Lake, passing the city of Whitestone along the way.

As the river makes it’s way south, it gains strength from countless streams and smaller rivers that flow down from the Alabasters along the way. It roars through the Parchwood Timberland and then widens and slows through the eastern Dividing Plains.

The Upper Mooren River Run is barely navigable. There are numerous falls and intense rapids at various points along the river. It is most certainly a one-way trip with travel up-river impossible by non-magical means.

Canon Consistency: High - (Tal’Dorei Campaign Guide, p. 46)

Daggerbay

Exandria > Tal’Dorei > Bladeshimmer Shoreline

Type: Coastal & Underwater

Daggerbay is a small bay on the southern end of the Bladeshimmer Shoreline. The bay is sheltered from the rough waters of the southern Ozmit Sea, but the entrance is well guarded by the jagged and dangerous Slumber Reef.

The bay was once home to the colony of O’noa, the first permanent, human settlement on Tal’Dorei following the Calamity. After O’Noa was destroyed during the Scattered War, both the city and the bay were abandoned in favor of the already more prosperous Emon.

Over time, the bay became a haven for pirates and sahuagin with the occassional foolhardy treasure hunter looking to salvage treasure from the countless wrecks on the Slumber Reef.

Emerald River

Exandria > Tal’Dorei > Bladeshimmer Shoreline

Type: Grassland

The Emerald River is a shallow, rocky river that descends from the Daggerbay Mountains south of the Emerald Outpost and out onto the plains of the Bladeshimmer Shoreline. It travels west nearly 150 miles where it joins the Ivyheart Run before emptying into the Ozmit Sea.

The Emerald River is navigable via small boats for much of its lower course. It starts as a rushing torrent of water in the Daggerbay Mountains, but quickly widens and shallows as it hits the plains.

The Emerald Path follows the river for most of its course before diverging at the base of the mountains and heading due east to the Emerald Outpost.

Type: Jungle

Vos’Sykriss was a large city located on the larger of the two islands known as Visa Isle. It was the capital city and crowning jewel of a Yuan-Ti civilization and it dominated the coast of southwestern Tal’Dorei.

In the early 800s, an expedition from the Alabaster Lyceum explored the ruined city and brought back many artifacts. Shortly after their departure, the Yuan-Ti began to awaken from their slumber.

The Cliffkeep Mountains is a massive mountain range that covers nearly one-third of the entire continent of Tal’Dorei. The entire range stretches from the Dividing Plains in the center of the continent, all the way to the ice-cold Frigid Depths far to the north.

Due to its massive size, the Cliffkeeps are often subdivided into five distinct ranges.

Eastern Range

The eastern range of the Cliffkeeps runs along the western shore of the Dawnmist Bay from the Dawnmist Pines in the south, along the eastern side of the Black Valley to the Neverfields.

Gatshadow Spur

The Gatshadow Spur is a range that shoots down between the southern and eastern ranges towards the Dividing Plains. It frames the western side of the Black Valley and comes to an abrupt halt at the massive Gatshadow.

Northern Range

The northern range of the Cliffkeeps runs up through the center of the region, along the western edge of the Grey Valley to the Elvenpeaks and the city of Lyrengorn.

Southern Range

The southern range of the Cliffkeeps begins at the Othendin Pass and runs along the northern border of the Torian Forest and ends east of the dwarven city of Kraghammer at the start of the Gatshadow Spur.

Western Range

The western range of the Cliffkeeps is located in the nortwestern corner of the region. It includes the small chain west of the Othendin Pass, breaks north of Fort Daxio and continues north along the northwest coast of Tal’Dorei.

Canon Consistency: Medium - (Tal’Dorei Campaign Guide, p. 80)

Due to the sheer enormity of the Cliffkeep Mountains, the various ranges are known by many names to many peoples. While these ranges are not identified on any previous maps, I decided to adopt the logical names for these ranges as given by the dwarves of Kraghammer and translated into Common.

Ruins of Wittebak

Exandria > Tal’Dorei > Cliffkeep Mountains

Type: Mountain & Underdark

Located beneath the Pools of Wittebak is the ruined, gnomish city of Wittebak. Once home to more than three thousand gnomes, Wittebak was destroyed around 415 PD when a clan of hill giants caused a crack to vent noxious gases into the city while they played in the pools above. Those that survived, moved to Kraghammer where they were welcomed, somewhat reluctantly by the insular dwarves.

The city is divided into three tiers, each with it’s own challenges.

Tinkerer’s Tier. The topmost level of Wittebak is breathable, but only to a point. A creature not accustomed to the environment can stand a number of hours equal to their constitution modifier before they start taking 1d6 poison damage for each minute beyond that.

Local hill giants have adapted to the gas and move about the top tier freely.

Homesteads. The second level is much more noxious and anyone down longer than an hour begins taking damage. The entire level is like a tomb with many of the long dead gnomes still interred here. Water leaks from the pools and falls above.

Engine Core. The lowest level is full of complex gearworks and industrial wonders, but it is flooded and crawling with merrow and is home to a sea hag named Mauvlettir and her lair.

Lower Mooren River Run

Exandria > Tal’Dorei > Lucidian Coast

Type: Coastal

Unlike the upper, the lower Mooren River Run is a wide, deep, slow moving channel that connects the Mooren Lake and the lakeside town of Drynna in eastern Tal’Dorei to the Lucidian Ocean. It provides a second, albeit smaller, port for the Republic of Tal’Dorei on the Lucidian Coast and it is an important trade route with the Menagerie Coast on the continent of Wildemount.

The lower Mooren River Run is a little over 100 miles long and is deep and wide enough for keelboats and schooners to navigate upriver to the Mooren Lake. The shore on either side of the river is coastal plain, with the easter bank a little wetter as it slowly transitions to the Rootgarden Marsh on the eastern side of the lake.

Trips up and down the lower Mooren are slow going and typically require extra security to keep pirates and opportunistic gnolls or lizardfolk from raiding the ships as they make the trip upriver.

Rumors speak of multiple hordes stashed throughout the vast network of tunnels that twist and wind through the heart of the mountain. Among them are the Obsidian Geode and the Everflame Crevasse, two of the richest and those the tribes fight over constantly.
